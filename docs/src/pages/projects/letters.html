《Letters》2024

In today’s age of advanced digital communication, letters are rarely used for everyday interaction. Most physical mail now exists in the form of formal documents or official notices. As a tangible medium, a letter must be physically delivered to the recipient’s location to be read, making the transmission slower and more prone to loss compared to digital messages. However, when words and thoughts are transformed into a physical letter, the result is a communication medium that carries a deeper sense of intimacy and emotional connection.

The work Letter involves overlapping papercuts on postcards to form fragmented written messages. Each piece contains separate, broken text. The cut-outs and negative space created by the papercuts divide radicals and letters, and the recipient must physically align the two layers to fully read the message. In this piece, I experiment with deconstructing language from its function as a vessel of meaning, turning words into something like eraser shavings—fragments that aren’t simply erased but compressed, crumpled, and left as residue. The desire to express becomes a process of reflection before writing, with the imagined presence of the recipient guiding the message—someone with whom I share a bond from the past, now addressed in the present with wishes for the future.

Recipients include long-lost friends, deceased family members, and overseas acquaintances. This project will continue as a long-term practice. I firmly believe that the difference between handwritten letters and digital text goes beyond speed and convenience. The few grams of weight in a letter can serve as a substitute for the physical body that cannot be present beside the person on the other end.
